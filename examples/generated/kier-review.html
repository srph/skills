Option Review

Notion vs Linear Docs

A product comparison for a startup team choosing where to keep decisions, specs, meeting notes, and lightweight project documentation.

Conditional winner

Pick Notion for a broad company knowledge base.

Pick Linear Docs when documentation should stay close to issues, projects, and product execution.

Strengths And Weaknesses

Option profiles

Best all-purpose workspace

Notion

Strengths
  • Excellent for mixed company knowledge: notes, policies, specs, databases, and dashboards.
  • Highly customizable, with enough templates for non-technical teams.
  • Works well when documentation lives beyond product and engineering.
Weaknesses
  • Easy to overbuild and hard to keep tidy without ownership.
  • Product work can drift away from the issue tracker.
  • Power comes with more decisions about structure.
Best product-team companion

Linear Docs

Strengths
  • Keeps specs, decisions, and project context near the work.
  • Lower overhead for teams already living in Linear.
  • Cleaner default shape for product and engineering docs.
Weaknesses
  • Less suitable as a whole-company knowledge base.
  • Fewer flexible database-style workflows.
  • Value drops if the team does not use Linear heavily.

Use-Case Verdict

Choose based on where the review will be used

Choose Notion

For a shared company library that includes people ops, sales notes, strategy, project plans, and recurring meeting docs.

Choose Linear Docs

For specs, product decisions, roadmap context, and documentation that should sit directly beside execution work.

No clear winner

If the team needs both a company wiki and execution-native specs, use Notion for broad knowledge and Linear Docs for active product work.

Final verdict: Notion is the stronger default for broad organizational memory. Linear Docs wins for product teams that want documentation to stay close to planning and delivery.
